Terminology

Saturation
The intensity and purity of a color. Can be used to make colors more vivid or muted.
Hue
The purest form of a color.
Color Palette
The set of available colors and image could be made.
Color Wheel
A fundamental tool in color theory. Illustrates the relationship between colors, from opposites to companions.
Primary Colors
Pure and unable to be created by mixing other colors. Includes red, blue, and yellow.
Secondary Colors
Colors formed by mixing two primary colors. Includes green (blue and yellow), violet (red and blue), and orange (red and yellow).
Tertiary Colors
Colors formed by mixing a primary and a secondary color together. Includes but not limited to blue-green and red-violet.
Triadic Colors
A group of three colors evenly spaced out on the color wheel. The primary colors are a example of this.
Complementary Colors
The colors on opposite ends of the color wheel. Violet and yellow or blue and orange are an example of this.
Split Colors
An evolution of cplementary colors. Instead of violet and yellow, its violet, yellow-green, and yellow-orange. Or red-violet, green, and yellow.
Analogous Colors
A group of colors, typically three, right next to eachother on a color wheel. Blue, blue-green, green is an example.
Color Modes
The meaning or symbolism of a color as defined by a culture. Western and eastern cultures can vary drastically and should be taken into account when building color schemes.
Monochrome
A color scheme of various shades, tones, and tints of a singular hue. Provides visual cohension and a harmonious look.
RGB
Standa from Red, Green, and Blue. How color is displayed digital displays.
CMYK
Stands for Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black (Key). How color is displayed in print.
Warm Colors
Colors that bring energy and intensity.
Cool Colors
Colors that brings to mind calmness or a serene feeling.
Color Scheme
A series of colors that work together to evoke a feeling or convey a message.
Vibrancy
The brightness, intensity, and energy of a hue.
Contrast
The impact of combining different hues in a way that highlights their differences and thus enhancing their impact.
