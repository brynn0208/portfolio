Terminology relating to typography.

Terminology

Ascender Line
Part of the lowercase letters that goes above the x-height in a typeface.
Descender Line
Part of lowecase letetrs that go below the baseline in a typeface.
Baseline
The imaginary line where the letters of a text rests.
Cap Height
The height of a capital letter in a particular typeface above the baseline.
X-Height
The height equivalent to the lowercase letter x in a typeface. Between the baseline and midline.
Serif
A traditional typeface. Marked by small decorative lines at the "feet" at the ends of characters. Favored for printed, text-heavy material.
Sans Serif
A modern and minimalistic looking typeface. Marked by clean characters. Used in both digital and print.
Decroative
A whimsical or eccentric looking typeface. Used for logos and headers, but should be used in moderation.
Script
Typefaces that aim to mimic fluid handwriting. Used for decroative purposes, such as branding.
Monoscape
Typefaces that occupy the same horizontal space. Used in typewriters and coding.
